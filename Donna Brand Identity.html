01 — Logo
The Wordmark

No symbol. No icon. The name, set in Playfair Display 700, followed by a single Dawn full stop. The period is the brand — a mark of finality, confidence, and completion. Done.

01
Primary — on dark Use the parchment wordmark on charcoal (#2D2D2D) or any dark surface. Dawn period remains unchanged.
02
Primary — on light Use the charcoal wordmark on parchment (#FAF9F6), white, or linen (#F1EDEA) surfaces.
03
Icon mark D + period used at small sizes (favicon, app icon, avatar). Never shrink the full wordmark below 80px wide.
04
Clear space Maintain clear space equal to the height of the capital D on all four sides. Never add a box, border, or background shape.

Dawn is singular
Dawn is the only chromatic colour in the brand. Never introduce new accent colours. Its restraint is what makes it powerful — when Dawn appears, something matters.
Charcoal, not black
#2D2D2D has warmth. True black (#000000) is never used. This keeps the brand feeling editorial and considered rather than stark or clinical.
White lifts, linen grounds
White (#FFFFFF) is for card surfaces that need to lift off the page. Linen (#F1EDEA) is for panels that should feel settled and warm. Parchment is the page itself.
Slate is the voice of context
Metadata, labels, captions, and secondary text all use Slate (#8D99AE). It reads clearly without competing with the primary content layer.

06 — Usage Rules
Do and Don't

The Editor brand has restraint at its core. These rules preserve the signal-to-noise ratio the brand depends on.

Do
✓
Use the full wordmark "Donna." with the Dawn period as a single unit
✓
Use Playfair Display 700 for all headlines and display text
✓
Keep Dawn for actions, urgency, and the logo mark only
✓
Use generous white space — the restraint in the design signals restraint in the AI
✓
Write copy that is direct, dry, and already one step ahead
✓
Use JetBrains Mono for all metadata, labels, timestamps, and system status
Don't
✗
Never use the wordmark without the Dawn period — it is not optional
✗
Never introduce additional accent colours alongside Dawn
✗
Never use true black (#000) or pure white — always charcoal and parchment
✗
Never use Playfair Display at weights below 400 or for body copy
✗
Never write copy that hedges, pads, or softens — Donna doesn't flinch
✗
Never add drop shadows, gradients, or decorative flourishes to the wordmark
